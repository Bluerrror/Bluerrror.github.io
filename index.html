Key Research & Publications

My research focuses on applying statistical, numerical, and machine learning models to solve complex problems in hydrology and environmental science. Use the filters below to explore publications related to specific topics. Click "View Evidence" to see supporting visuals from the research.

Soil Erosion in the United States (Present & Future)

Estimated and predicted water-driven soil erosion rates across the contiguous U.S. for the present and future (2020-2050) using the G2 erosion model integrated with ML and RS techniques.

Stochastic Modeling of Sediment Load in Alluvial Rivers

Introduced a parsimonious probabilistic model rooted in multivariate probability theory and Bayesian Networks to describe the relationship between Suspended Sediment Load (SSL) and discharge volume.

SSL Modeling using Hydro-Climate Variables & Machine Learning

Investigated the efficacy of ML models (Xgboost, LightGBM, Catboost) in modeling sediment transport using hydro-climate variables like streamflow, precipitation, and temperature as input data.

Probabilistic Hazard Assessment of Contaminants

Proposed a probabilistic framework rooted in multivariate and Copula theory to assess heavy metal hazard associated with contaminated sediment in freshwater rivers.

Process-constrained Sediment Yield Modeling

Presented a framework to fine-tune a stochastic sediment yield model by classifying discharge and SSL observations based on underlying governing processes in unregulated streams.

Road Construction Effects on Turbidity

Used Sentinel-1 and Sentinel-2 data in Google Earth Engine (GEE) for a spatio-temporal analysis of turbidity in water bodies adjacent to a highway construction project in Norway.

Historical Hazard Assessment in Oman

Presented a novel high-resolution (30 m) soil erosion framework using the G2 model, integrating satellite datasets and ML models to assess spatiotemporal soil erosion risks over four decades.

2D Velocity Distribution with Entropy

Used a General Index Entropy-based method to derive a two-dimensional velocity distribution profile in open channels by maximizing entropy and treating velocity as a random variable.
