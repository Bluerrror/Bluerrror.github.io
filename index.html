Academic & Professional Synopsis

As a Research Habilitation Candidate in Water Resources Engineering at the University of Kassel, my scholarship integrates stochastic hydrology, geospatial machine learning, and process-based environmental modeling to address pressing challenges in sediment transport, soil conservation, and food security under climate variability. With a Ph.D. from Shiraz University (2021) on Copula-based sediment yield prediction—published in CATENA (IF: 6.187)—I have pioneered parsimonious probabilistic frameworks that outperform traditional deterministic models by 25-40% in predictive accuracy across 67 U.S. watersheds. My postdoctoral tenure at Sultan Qaboos University (2021-2023) advanced high-resolution erosion hazard mapping for Omani arid systems, fusing Sentinel-2 data with XGBoost ensembles to inform national land-use policy. Currently, through the DigiPlus initiative, I deploy hybrid crop-yield forecasting pipelines leveraging Google Earth Engine and deep neural architectures to enhance digital agriculture resilience. My methodological ethos emphasizes open-source reproducibility (e.g., GitHub repositories with >500 stars) and interdisciplinary synthesis, bridging hydraulic engineering with data science to yield actionable, uncertainty-aware solutions for sustainable resource management.
